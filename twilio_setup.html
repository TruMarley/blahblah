1
Create a Twilio Account
⏱ ~3 minutes
▾

Twilio handles all the phone infrastructure — incoming calls, SMS, forwarding. You'll get $15 free credit to start.

  • 1Go to twilio.com and click "Start for Free"
  • 2Create your account with your email and verify your phone number
  • 3In the Twilio Console, find your Account SID and Auth Token on the homepage dashboard
  • 4Add these to your .env file: TWILIO_ACCOUNT_SID=ACxxx and TWILIO_AUTH_TOKEN=xxx
# .env file
TWILIO_ACCOUNT_SID=ACxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx
TWILIO_AUTH_TOKEN=xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx
TWILIO_PHONE_NUMBER=+17865550000
DEEPGRAM_API_KEY=xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx
💡 Your Account SID starts with "AC" — it's 34 characters long. Never share your Auth Token publicly.
🖥️
Twilio Console homepage — Account SID and Auth Token
2
Buy a Local Phone Number
⏱ ~2 minutes
▾

Buy a local number in your city. Clients will see this number when your AI calls or texts them. Use the $15 free trial credit.

  • 1In Twilio Console → Phone Numbers → Manage → Buy a Number
  • 2Search by your area code (e.g., 305 for Miami) — filter by Voice + SMS
  • 3Pick a number and click "Buy" (~$1.15/month)
  • 4Add TWILIO_PHONE_NUMBER=+1XXXXXXXXXX to your .env file
📱
Twilio phone number search — filter by area code
ℹ️ During Twilio trial, you can only call/text verified numbers. Upgrade to a paid account before going live with customers.
3
Configure the Incoming Call Webhook
⏱ ~5 minutes
▾

Tell Twilio where to send incoming calls — your RingDesk server. You'll use ngrok to expose your local server to the internet for testing.

First, start ngrok:

ngrok http 5050
💡 ngrok gives you a public URL like https://abc123.ngrok.io — copy that URL.
  • 1In Twilio Console → Phone Numbers → Manage → Active Numbers
  • 2Click your phone number to edit it
  • 3Under "A call comes in" → set to Webhook
  • 4Paste your ngrok URL + /incoming-call:
    https://abc123.ngrok.io/incoming-call
  • 5Set method to HTTP POST → Save
🔗
Twilio Phone Number config — "A call comes in" webhook
⚠️ ngrok URL changes every restart unless you have a paid plan. For production, deploy RingDesk to a server (Railway, Fly.io, DigitalOcean) and use that URL instead.
4
Forward Your Existing Number to Twilio
⏱ ~5 minutes
▾

Keep your current business number. Set it to forward to your Twilio number when you're busy or after hours. Your AI answers the forwarded calls automatically.

Option A: Forward on No Answer (recommended)

  • 1On most carriers, dial **61*+1XXXXXXXXXX# (replace with your Twilio number) to forward unanswered calls
  • 2To cancel forwarding: dial ##61#

Option B: Forward All Calls

  • 1On Android: Settings → Phone → Call Forwarding → Always Forward
  • 2On iPhone: Contact your carrier (AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile) — they set it in their app
  • 3Enter your Twilio number as the forward destination
📲
Phone call forwarding settings — forward to your Twilio number

Test your setup:

# Make sure RingDesk is running first:
cd ~/voice_ai && source venv/bin/activate && python3 server.py

# Then call your Twilio number from another phone.
# You should hear your AI receptionist answer!
💡 Pro tip: Call your own Twilio number from a second phone. You should hear your AI greet you within 1-2 seconds.

🎉 You're live!

Your AI receptionist is now answering calls 24/7. Check your dashboard to see incoming bookings.
